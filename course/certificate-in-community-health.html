Certificate Course in Community Health

The Certificate Course in Community Health in association with NIOS is carried out at Expert Paramedical Institute & Research Center will be a 1 year full-term course. The different subjects that the students would be exposed be during the tenure of the course our as follows:

  • Human Anatomy and Physiology
  • Health and Hygiene
  • Our Body and Immune System
  • Prevention of Common Diseases and Home Remedies
  • Yoga and Health
  • Management of Diseases through Yoga

 Eligibility Criteria

  • Candidates must have finished B.Sc. Nursing/ Ayurveda Graduates with BAMS, Zoology, Botany, etc. from acknowledged institutes or universities are eligible.
  • Candidates with GNM or Post Basic B.Sc. Nurses from any recognized institute are also eligible

Syllabus:

  • Human Anatomy and Physiology
  • Health and Hygiene
  • Our Body and Immune System
  • Prevention of Common Diseases and Home Remedies
  • Yoga and Health
  • Management of Diseases through Yoga

Job Opportunities

 1. Community Health Worker

    Provide outreach, education, and support to individuals and communities regarding health and wellness. Assist in navigating healthcare systems and resources.

 2. Health Educator

    Develop and implement programs and materials to educate the public about health issues, prevention strategies, and healthy behaviours.

 3. Public Health Assistant

    Support public health programs and initiatives, assist with data collection, and contribute to the development of community health strategies.

 4. Patient Navigator

    Help patients understand and access healthcare services, manage their healthcare plans, and provide support throughout their treatment journey.

 5. Health Promotion Specialist

    Design and implement health promotion campaigns and programs to improve community health outcomes.

 6. Disease Prevention Specialist

    Focus on preventing the spread of diseases through education, immunization programs, and community outreach.

 7. Emergency Preparedness Coordinator

    Develop and implement plans for emergency preparedness and response, focusing on community health and safety during crises.

 8. Health Services Coordinator

    Coordinate and manage health services and resources in community settings, ensuring accessibility and efficiency.

 9. Social and Human Services Assistant

    Assist individuals in accessing social services and health resources, and provide support in achieving personal and community health goals.

 10. Health Policy Advocate

    Work on developing, advocating for, and implementing policies that promote public health and address health disparities.

 11. Community Outreach Coordinator

    Plan and execute outreach programs to engage community members in health-related initiatives and services.

 12. Research Assistant in Public Health

    Assist with research projects related to community health issues, data collection, and analysis.
