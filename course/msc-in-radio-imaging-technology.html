MSc in Radio Imaging Technology Course

Radiography and imaging technology direct the use of various imaging techniques in the medical sector to produce images of the human body for diagnostic and treatment purposes. An investigation in radiographic imaging technology would cover various imaging Instruments such as X-ray, Computed Tomography (CT), Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI), Positron Emission Tomography (PET), etc

One of the top Expert Paramedical Institute & Research Center in BhadohiNCR has brought the MSc. RIT course for all those candidates who are ready to join this course and launch their Careers.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Candidates are eligible to apply for the MSc in Radiology Imaging Technology after the completion of Graduation with a minimum of 50% marks in relevant subjects. 
  • The candidate has to be a graduate of any Science subject.
  • These criteria may also vary in Institutes and universities.

Syllabus

The Course is divided into two parts.

Part - I

  • Management of Radiology and Imaging Department.
  • Modern Radiological and Imaging Equipment and Techniques.
  • Advanced Physic of Radiology and Imaging.

Practical activities are provided about the radiological equipment and the radiographic techniques of specialised cases.

Part - II 

  • Quality Assurance and Quality Control in the Radiology and Imaging Department.
  • Newer Imaging Modalities and Intervention Radiological Techniques.
  • Radiation Safety and Protection with Radiological Procedures.

This course mainly covers topics such as:

  • The codes and principles of medical imaging and radiation physics
  • Image acquisition techniques, including patient positioning and exposure parameters
  • Image processing and investigation, including digital image processing and computer-aided diagnosis
  • Image arrangement, including the distinction between normal and abnormal findings
  • Medical imaging equipment, including X-ray machines, CT scanners, and MRI machines
  • Radiation safety, including the safe use of ionising radiation in medical imaging and patient safety

After graduating from this course you may seek professions as Radiologic technologists, sonographers, and radiation therapists.

Job opportunities

X-ray imaging: This is a mostly used imaging technology that utilises ionising radiation to create images of bones and inner organs. One controls the exposure of the human body to X-rays and with the lower exposure delivers precise images of the human body.

Computed Tomography (CT): CT scans utilise X-rays and computer processing to create clear cross-sectional body images of the patient

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI): MRI utilises a magnetic field and Radiography waves to create pictures of the body's inner Parts. With the help of magnetic fields 3d images are developed for the complete diagnosis of the patient.

Positron Emission Tomography (PET): PET scans use radioactive tracers to create images of part of the organ that is affected. 

Nuclear medicine: This is one of the most specialised fields that uses Radioactive materials to produce images of the body's organs and tissues and assess their function. Doctors use nuclear medicine tests to diagnose and treat different conditions. These include cancer, heart disease, gastrointestinal, endocrine, and neurological disorders. 

Medical Imaging Specialist: Work in hospitals, clinics, or research institutions to operate and help medical imaging equipment such as MRI, CT scanners, ultrasound machines, and X-ray systems. You may also be involved in examinations to enhance imaging techniques and diagnostic capabilities.

 Research Scientist: Conduct research in academia, government agencies, or private research institutions to promote the field of radio and imaging technology. This could involve creating new imaging techniques, improving signal processing algorithms, or exploring novel applications of radio waves.

Project Manager: Lead teams involved in the development, implementation, or maintenance of radio and imaging technology projects. This role demands strong technical knowledge incorporated with project management skills to ensure projects are completed on time and within budget.

Consultant: Provide expertise to organizations seeking to integrate or optimize radio and imaging technology solutions. Consultants may work independently or for consulting firms, advising clients on technology adoption, system design, and regulatory compliance.

Quality Assurance Engineer: Confirm that radio and imaging equipment and systems meet regulatory standards and performance requirements. This role involves testing, troubleshooting, and validating systems to ensure they operate reliably and safely.

 Sales Engineer: Combine technical knowledge with sales skills to promote and sell radio and imaging technology products and solutions. Sales engineers work closely with customers to understand their requirements and suggest appropriate solutions.

Academic or Industry Trainer: Teach courses or provide training workshops on radio and imaging technology concepts, applications, and practical skills. This role can be in academic institutions, technical training centres, or as part of corporate training programs.
